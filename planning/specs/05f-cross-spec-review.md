# Spec 05f — Cross-Spec Consistency Review (whole-suite)

**Status:** v1.0 — July 2026 — consolidated consistency review by Claude Fable 5 across the *complete* spec suite.
**Scope reviewed:** Specs 01–04 in full; the 05 series (05 sampled at the sections other specs bind to — §3, §13, §15, §24 — plus 05b and 05c in full); Specs 06–11 in full (the six written in parallel by independent agents, whose mutual coherence had not yet been checked); research doc §12–§15.
**Method:** read in dependency order; checked every cross-spec citation named in one spec against the cited section in the other; collected every closed enum defined in more than one place and diffed them; consolidated, deduped, and validated the tensions the six parallel authors self-reported in their own Decision Records/Open lists; and swept for concerns claimed by two specs or by none. This is a *report*, not an edit pass — nothing outside this file was changed. Where a finding was already self-reported by an owning spec, that is credited (a self-reported, correctly-dispositioned tension is not a defect; an *unreconciled* one is).
**Relation to 05c/05e:** 05c reviewed research + Specs 01–05 and edited in place; this review covers the seams 05c could not see — 06↔11 against each other and against the 01–05 core — and reports only.

---

## 0. Overall coherence assessment

**Verdict: the suite is internally consistent enough to build from, with one structural hole (Voice), one pre-conditioned code fix (deviceId), and roughly a dozen reconciliation edits that should be batched into a single "suite-sync" pass before any spec section is treated as normative in isolation.**

What the whole-suite reading shows, positively:

1. **The six parallel specs converged far more than they diverged.** The three heavyweight shared concerns — the plaintext/deferred-encryption posture (`G-37`), the single cloud seam, and act-then-describe — are stated compatibly in every spec that touches them. Where two of the new specs cover the same ground (turnlog placement: 06 §10.3, 10 R-02, 11 D9; sensitive-skill exclusion: 08 §5.2/Q6, 10 R-05; the 20/hour rate cap: 03 §3.5 banner, 08 Q1), they independently reached the **same** conclusion — a strong signal the underlying design is sound and the specs were written from it rather than past each other.
2. **The suite's self-knowledge is good.** Most of the contradictions below were flagged by at least one of the involved specs (06 Q8, 07 X4, 08 D3/Q7, 09 O8, 10 R-02–R-06, 11 D9). The failure mode is not ignorance; it is that each spec *recorded* the reconciliation on its own side and the counterpart spec was never edited — so a reader of Spec 04 alone still builds Sonnet authoring, and a reader of Spec 02 alone still believes three safety layers ship in v1.
3. **The genuinely dangerous items are few and known.** Two are code-side (deviceId and turnlog in the synced folder — §2, CS-01/CS-02); one is a status-honesty gap in a safety section (CS-07); one is a missing spec (Voice, CS-03).

The characteristic defect class of this suite is **"reconciled in one home, stale in the other"** — Spec 06 reassigns `lastModified`'s three roles but Specs 01/02/04 still state the old ones; Spec 08 D3 supersedes Spec 04's "Sonnet" docstring but Spec 04 still says Sonnet; Spec 07 X4 identifies Spec 04's subtitle miscite but Spec 04 still miscites. None of these is a design disagreement; all of them are implementer traps. The recommended remedy is one mechanical pass (see §5) applying the already-recorded resolutions to their counterpart specs, rather than re-opening any decision.
